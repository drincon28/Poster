RHYTHME
Web music rhythm assistant focused on kids between 4 and 12 years old with arduino multipad
Diana Paola Rincón - drincon28@uan.edu.co
Wilmer Rodríguez - wrodriguez462@uan.edu.co

Director:Wilson Forero - wilson.forero@uan.edu.co
Context


- Music is one of the intelligences: H.G.
- Is an Art, the expression of our souls.
- Rhythm is present everywhere, in our lives, his learning gives us advantages in multiple human activities.
Question

How could we implement a free portal to support rhythmic musical learning?
Problem
  - Expensive tools
- No accessible technology
- No training feedback without tutor
- Loud percusion instruments
- Boring methodology

Kids face these among other
problems when they are trying
to learn or practice their exercises.

There's no open platform where
you can practice your own rhythm
patterns, also the digital instruments
available are expensive, not easy to handle, and doesn't have option to program your patterns.

Tutors and their time are a limited resource, and the feedback they gives to the student is extremely important.
Main Objective


- To implement a free portal using modern web tools, for the rhythmic autonomous musical learning of children between 4 and 12 years old
Objectives


- To develop a site with web gaming modern tools to practice rhythm musical excercises.
- To expland the player with instant feedback on every hit the player success/miss.
- To create a full media tutorial DIY to build a keyboard/arduino multipad.
- To deliver multimedia definitions about rhythm in a full site kids-friendly.
